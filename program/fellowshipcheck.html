Fellowship

Initial Research is a meeting space for creative talents, fostering a continuous exchange of experiences and ideas. At its core, the Initial Research Fellowship provides a select group of creatives with the resources to pursue a self-directed, yearlong research project. Fellows receive access to office and project space, where they develop their work through a series of gatherings, including workshops, lectures, screenings, and reading groups. Each participant will present their research internally and will organize public-facing events.

01 te editions (Michael Guo & Kechun Qin)

te editions

Michael Guo & Kechun Qin

Independent publishing and research studio exploring the intersection of art, design, and technology. Their work focuses on creating experimental publications and digital experiences that challenge traditional forms of knowledge dissemination. Through their fellowship, they aim to investigate new models of collaborative publishing that connect artists, designers, and technologists across disciplines.

Founded in 2020, te editions has collaborated with various cultural institutions, artists, and researchers to produce publications that exist at the intersection of print and digital media. Their approach emphasizes process-based research and experimentation with new publishing formats.

02 Nini Dongnier

Nini Dongnier

Visual Artist

Nini Dongnier is a multimedia artist whose practice explores cultural identity, displacement, and collective memory through video, installation, and performance. Her work often incorporates archival materials and personal narratives to examine the intersection of individual experiences with broader social and historical contexts.

Born in Shanghai and based between New York and Berlin, Dongnier's practice is informed by her experiences navigating between cultural contexts. She holds an MFA from Columbia University and has exhibited her work internationally.

03 Sowon Kwon

Sowon Kwon

Artist and Educator

Sowon Kwon is an interdisciplinary artist whose work spans drawing, digital animation, video, and installation. Her practice investigates the construction of cultural identities and the politics of representation through a feminist lens, often incorporating text, historical research, and pop cultural references.

Kwon is an Associate Professor at Parsons School of Design and has exhibited her work at the Whitney Museum, the Queens Museum, and the Berkeley Art Museum, among others. Her writing has been published in journals such as Women & Performance and Documents.

04 Yu Ji & Ho King Man

Yu Ji & Ho King Man

Collaborative Artists

Yu Ji and Ho King Man are a collaborative duo working at the intersection of spatial practice, sound, and performance. Their projects often respond to specific architectural contexts, creating site-responsive installations that alter viewers' perception of space through subtle interventions.

Based between Hong Kong and London, they have developed projects for institutions including the Tai Kwun Contemporary, Para Site, and the Institute of Contemporary Arts London. Their work has been supported by the Hong Kong Arts Development Council and the British Council.
